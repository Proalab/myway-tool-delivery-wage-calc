Understanding Your Hidden Costs

Many delivery drivers ignore these costs because they don't pay them immediately. But eventually, the bill comes due.

1. Depreciation (The Silent Killer)

Every mile you drive lowers the resale value of your vehicle.

The Math: If you bought a van for $30,000 and it lasts 200,000 miles, it costs you $0.15 per mile just in lost value.

The Reality: When you trade in your vehicle later, you will get thousands less because of those high delivery miles. Our calculator estimates this so you can save for your next vehicle.

2. The Self-Employment Tax Hit

As a W-2 employee, your employer pays half your Social Security and Medicare taxes. As an independent contractor (1099), you must pay both halves.

The Rate: The Self-Employment Tax rate is roughly 15.3% of your net profit.

The Trap: Most new drivers spend this money, only to panic when tax season arrives in April. This tool sets that money aside in the calculation.

3. Maintenance & Repairs

Tires, oil changes, and brake pads wear out faster when you drive delivery routes‚Äîespecially with frequent stops and heavy loads.

Rule of Thumb: You should budget roughly $0.05 to $0.10 per mile for future repairs. Cargo vans and larger vehicles cost more to maintain than sedans.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is Amazon Flex worth it in 2026?

It depends on your vehicle and market. If you drive a gas-guzzling van or SUV, you might actually be making less than minimum wage after expenses. If you drive a fuel-efficient car for smaller routes, your profit margins are much higher. Use the calculator above to check your specific setup.

What is the 2026 IRS Mileage Rate?

The standard IRS mileage rate for 2026 is 72.5 cents per mile. You can deduct this amount from your taxable income, which helps lower your tax bill significantly. However, a tax deduction is not the same as "profit"‚Äîyou still spent money on the vehicle.

Which delivery service pays the best for package delivery?

It varies by region. Amazon Flex offers flexible blocks but inconsistent pay. FedEx Ground contractors often earn more per route but require longer commitments. UPS and USPS W-2 positions typically offer better benefits but are harder to get. Compare your true hourly pay across platforms.

How can I increase my true hourly pay?

  1. 1. Choose the Right Vehicle: A fuel-efficient cargo van or hatchback can save you hundreds per month.
  2. 2. Optimize Your Route: Plan your stops efficiently to minimize miles driven per package.
  3. 3. Pick Better Blocks: For Amazon Flex, learn which warehouse locations and time slots pay better for the miles involved.
  4. 4. Track Everything: Use apps like Stride or Everlance to track every mile. You cannot deduct miles you don't document.

Am I an employee or independent contractor?

Amazon Flex drivers are independent contractors (1099). FedEx Ground uses contracted service providers who may hire you as W-2 or 1099. UPS and USPS typically hire W-2 employees. If you're 1099, you're responsible for self-employment taxes‚Äîfactor that into your calculations.

How much do Amazon Flex drivers make per hour?

Amazon Flex advertises rates of $18-25 per hour, but this is your gross pay before expenses. After accounting for gas, depreciation, maintenance, and self-employment taxes, most drivers actually earn $10-15 per hour. Use our calculator above to find your real number based on your specific vehicle and market.

How many miles do delivery drivers drive per day?

The average delivery driver drives 100-150 miles per day, depending on the route density and area. Rural routes can exceed 200 miles, while dense urban routes might be under 80 miles. Every mile costs you money in gas, depreciation, and wear‚Äîso minimizing unnecessary miles through route optimization directly increases your profit.

What is the best car for Amazon Flex and package delivery?

The ideal delivery vehicle balances fuel efficiency, cargo space, and low depreciation. Top choices include: Toyota Prius (best MPG), Honda Fit (great cargo for size), Toyota Corolla Hatchback, or for larger routes, a used cargo van like Ford Transit Connect. Avoid large SUVs and trucks‚Äîtheir poor MPG will eat your profits.

What expenses can delivery drivers write off on taxes?

As a 1099 contractor, you can deduct: mileage (72.5¬¢/mile in 2026), phone and data plan (business portion), insulated bags, parking and tolls, and any supplies needed for deliveries. You can choose between the standard mileage deduction OR actual expenses (gas, insurance, repairs, depreciation)‚Äîbut not both. Most drivers benefit more from the standard mileage rate.

How do I track mileage for delivery driving?

The IRS requires documentation of every mile you deduct. Use a mileage tracking app like Stride, Everlance, or the built-in tracking in MyWay Route Planner. Record the date, starting/ending odometer, destination, and business purpose. Without proper records, you cannot claim the deduction‚Äîpotentially costing you thousands in tax savings.

How do I plan delivery routes more efficiently?

Standard GPS apps like Google Maps only give turn-by-turn directions‚Äîthey don't optimize the order of your stops. A dedicated route planning app analyzes all your stops and finds the most efficient sequence, reducing total miles by 20-30%. For a 100-stop route, this can save 30+ minutes and $10-15 in vehicle costs daily.

Is delivery driving hard on your car?

Yes. Delivery driving involves frequent stops, starts, and idling‚Äîwhich accelerates wear on brakes, transmission, and engine. Expect to replace brakes 2-3x more often than normal driving. Budget $0.05-0.10 per mile for maintenance. The best way to reduce wear is to minimize miles driven per delivery using optimized routes.

How much does gas cost for delivery drivers per month?

At 100 miles/day, 25 MPG, and $3.00/gallon, you'll spend about $360/month on gas alone. With a less efficient vehicle (18 MPG), that jumps to $500+/month. Gas is typically 30-40% of your total vehicle expenses. Reducing miles through efficient route planning and choosing a fuel-efficient vehicle are the two biggest levers to cut this cost.

Should I do Amazon Flex full-time or part-time?

Most financial advisors recommend starting part-time to test your true hourly pay before committing. Full-time Flex driving (40+ hours/week) accelerates vehicle depreciation and increases the risk of burnout. Many successful drivers combine Flex with other gig work or use it to supplement a primary income. Calculate your true pay first, then decide.

How do FedEx Ground contractors make money?

FedEx Ground contractors (called ISPs - Independent Service Providers) are paid per package and per stop, not hourly. They own the routes and hire drivers. As a driver for an ISP, your pay depends on your contractor‚Äîtypically $150-250/day for a full route. Some contractors provide vehicles; others require you to use your own. Always calculate your true hourly pay including vehicle costs.
