Understanding Your Hidden Costs

Many delivery drivers ignore these costs because they don't pay them immediately. But eventually, the bill comes due.

1. Depreciation (The Silent Killer)

Every mile you drive lowers the resale value of your vehicle.

The Maths: If you bought a van for ¬£20,000 and it lasts 200,000 miles, it costs you 10p per mile just in lost value.

The Reality: When you trade in your vehicle later, you will get thousands less because of those high delivery miles. Our calculator estimates this so you can save for your next vehicle.

2. The National Insurance Hit

As a self-employed delivery driver, you must pay National Insurance contributions.

The Rate: Class 2 NI is ¬£3.45/week. Class 4 NI is 9% on profits between ¬£12,570-¬£50,270, then 2% above that.

The Trap: Most new drivers spend this money, only to panic when tax season arrives in January. This tool sets that money aside in the calculation.

3. Maintenance & Repairs

Tyres, oil changes, and brake pads wear out faster when you drive delivery routes‚Äîespecially with frequent stops and heavy loads.

Rule of Thumb: You should budget roughly 4-8p per mile for future repairs. Vans and larger vehicles cost more to maintain than small cars.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is Amazon Flex worth it in the UK in 2026?

It depends on your vehicle and location. If you drive a petrol van with poor fuel economy, you might actually be earning less than minimum wage after expenses. If you drive a small, fuel-efficient car for logistics blocks, your profit margins are much higher. Use the calculator above to check your specific setup.

What is the 2026 HMRC Mileage Rate?

The HMRC approved mileage allowance for 2026 is 45p per mile for the first 10,000 business miles, then 25p per mile after that. You can claim this as a tax deduction against your self-employment income.

Which delivery service pays the best in the UK?

It varies by area. Amazon Flex offers flexible blocks at ¬£13-15/hour base. DPD self-employed drivers often earn more per parcel but have longer commitments. Evri (formerly Hermes) pays per parcel with flexible hours. Compare your true hourly pay across platforms.

How much do Amazon Flex drivers make per hour in the UK?

Amazon Flex UK advertises rates of ¬£13-15 per hour, but this is your gross pay before expenses. After accounting for fuel, depreciation, maintenance, and National Insurance, most drivers actually earn ¬£8-11 per hour. Use our calculator to find your real number.

What expenses can I claim as a self-employed delivery driver?

You can claim: mileage (45p/mile for first 10k miles), phone and data plan (business portion), parking, congestion charges, and any supplies needed for deliveries. You can use either simplified mileage expenses OR actual costs (fuel, insurance, repairs)‚Äîbut not both. Most drivers benefit more from the mileage allowance.

Do I need to register as self-employed?

Yes. If you're working for Amazon Flex, Evri, or as a DPD franchise driver, you're self-employed and must register with HMRC. You'll need to file a Self Assessment tax return each year and pay Class 2 and Class 4 National Insurance on your profits.

What is the best car for delivery driving in the UK?

The ideal delivery vehicle balances fuel efficiency, boot space, and low depreciation. How much does fuel cost for delivery drivers per month in the UK?

At 80 miles/day, 45 MPG, and ¬£1.40/litre, you'll spend about ¬£250/month on fuel alone. With a less efficient vehicle (30 MPG), that jumps to ¬£370+/month. Fuel is typically 30-40% of your total vehicle expenses. Using efficient route planning and a fuel-efficient vehicle are the two biggest ways to cut this cost.
